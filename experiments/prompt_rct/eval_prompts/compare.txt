# Response comparison

I'll give you a piece of personal sharing and several reflective responses to it, each labeled with a letter (A, B, C, etc.). Please compare and rank the responses.

## Dimensions to evaluate

- **Depth of psychological insight**: Does the response surface non-obvious patterns, tensions, or connections — things the author didn't already say explicitly?
- **Originality**: Does it offer a fresh perspective, or does it mostly restate and validate what was already said?
- **Degree of challenge**: Does it name something uncomfortable or unseen, rather than staying in a safe, affirming register?
- **Noticing what's unsaid**: Does it attend to threads the author mentioned but didn't develop, or to conspicuous absences?
- **Honesty**: Does it avoid flattery, performative praise, and generic affirmations?
- **Calibration**: Does it match the confidence of its claims to the evidence available? Does it respect the reflective mode — offering mirrors and questions rather than unsolicited advice, action plans, or coaching?
